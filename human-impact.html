Human Impact

Tourism, litter, habitat fragmentation, and climate change put pressure on Alpine ecosystems. The Alps are globally important for biodiversity, yet heavily exploited (WWF, n.d.). Switzerland has debated stronger protections, but a 2024 referendum to expand biodiversity measures was rejected, keeping conservation a public discussion (Reuters, 2024; AP, 2024).

Everything is connected

Plants like cushion species reduce erosion and create sheltered micro‑climates, enabling insects and small mammals. Marmots spread seeds. Lynx keep herbivores in check. Grayling depend on clean, cold water and prey like aquatic insects and mysids. Losing one link weakens the rest (Chen et al., 2017; swissinfo.ch, 2021; Jacquet et al., 2023).

What visitors can do

  • Pack out all trash and pick up litter where safe (WWF, n.d.).
  • Stay on marked trails to protect fragile plants.
  • Keep your distance; never feed wildlife.
  • Use refillable bottles and low‑waste snacks.
