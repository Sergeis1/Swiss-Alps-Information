Plants

Arolla (Swiss stone) pine (Pinus cembra) — Five needled pine of the swiss alps that easily survives temperatures of −40 °C, it produces seeds irregularly, and can live for many centuries (Swiss National Park, n.d.).

European larch (Larix decidua) — A conifer that turns golden in autumn season; it grows fast and is known for being long lived, with a strong growth response to global warming to stone pine (JRC, n.d.; Obojes et al., 2022).

Cushion plants — Low, dense mats that enjoy wind, cold, and UV, and can become a home to other species by creating micro habitats (Chen et al., 2017; Yang et al., 2025).

Cirsium spinosissimum — Spiny flowers of rocky areas in the Alps, they are a key source of nectar for insects (Cirsium spinosissimum, 2025).
