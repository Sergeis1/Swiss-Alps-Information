Welcome

The Swiss Alps are among Europe’s richest biodiversity hotspots, home to hardy plants, iconic mammals, and even cold-adapted microbes. These species form tight ecological links, and many are sensitive to warming, habitat fragmentation, and tourism pressure (WWF, n.d.).

What you’ll learn

  • Key animals like the chamois, ibex, marmot, lynx, and grayling — and why they matter (Swiss National Park, n.d.; swissinfo.ch, 2021; Eawag, 2023).
  • Alpine plants such as arolla pine, larch, cushion plants, and spiniest thistle (Swiss National Park, n.d.; JRC, n.d.; Chen et al., 2017; Cirsium spinosissimum, 2025).
  • Cold-adapted microbes in alpine soils, including Crenarchaeota (Adamczyk et al., 2019; Zumsteg et al., 2012).
  • How litter and climate change threaten connected food webs, and what visitors can do (WWF, 2023; Reuters, 2024).
