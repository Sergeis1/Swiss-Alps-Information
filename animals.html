Animals

Chamois (Rupicapra rupicapra) — Sure‑footed antelope of cliffs and meadows; commonly observed above the treeline in Swiss National Park (Swiss National Park, n.d.).

Ibex (Capra ibex) — Iconic wild goat with long, curved horns that frequent higher, rocky elevations (Swiss National Park, n.d.).

Marmot (Marmota marmota) — Burrowing rodents that hibernate through long winters and act as prey, seed dispersers, and soil engineers (Swiss National Park, n.d.).

Lynx (Lynx lynx) — Reintroduced top predator that helps control herbivores; about ~250 individuals in Switzerland in two main populations (Breitenmoser, 1998; swissinfo.ch, 2021).

Arctic grayling (Thymallus thymallus) — Cold‑water fish of Alpine rivers and canals in Switzerland; sensitive to warming (Eawag, 2023; FishBase, 2017).

Opossum shrimps (Mysidae) — Tiny crustaceans in cold, deep lakes and peri‑Alpine waters; important in food webs and recorded in Lake Geneva as invasive winter swarms of Hemimysis anomala (Jacquet et al., 2023).

Asian houbara (Chlamydotis macqueenii) — A desert bustard; not native to the Alps, but occasionally reported in wider Europe as a rare vagrant. Included here only as a comparative “visitor” example; conservation status and range are centered in Central/Southwest Asia (IUCN, n.d.).

How animals connect

Predators like lynx regulate herbivores; marmots disperse seeds and feed raptors; mysids and aquatic insects feed grayling; and alpine vegetation stabilizes soils and supplies forage. These links keep the web stable.

Protect the Alps — pack out trash, stay on trails, respect wildlife.
